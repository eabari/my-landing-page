The Problem

Why most AI initiatives stall

Most AI initiatives don't fail because of bad technology. They fail because the business and the technology function cannot talk to each other.

🎯
Business leaders nod along — but can't act. Tech teams present roadmaps filled with acronyms. Leaders approve budgets they don't fully understand. Initiatives launch, then stall when priorities shift.
🔧
Tech teams build what's asked — not what's needed. Without a shared language, technology gets scoped around assumptions, not business outcomes. The result: solutions that work technically, but don't move the needle.
💡
The gap is the problem — not the technology. EB has sat on both sides of this table for 28 years. He knows why it breaks down. And he knows how to fix it.

About EB

28 years on the inside.
Now working for you.

EB didn't arrive at AI consulting from the outside. He spent nearly three decades as CIO — sitting at the table where technology decisions were made and defended in business terms.

He watched the same story play out across industries: a leadership team that knew transformation was urgent but couldn't translate intent into action. A technology team that could build anything but struggled to articulate what it meant for revenue, costs, or customers. Two worlds. One broken conversation.

The insight that built XdotO: most AI failures aren't technology failures. They're communication failures. The business doesn't understand what's possible. The tech team doesn't understand what the business actually needs. EB built his company to be the bridge — someone who speaks both languages fluently, having lived on both sides for nearly three decades.

He is an IIT Kharagpur graduate with an MBA from SP Jain Institute of Management. He has served as CIO at a major credit card company, a leading life insurance firm, a large NBFC, a national QSR chain with 1,800+ locations, and a national media group — and has worked with a global industrial conglomerate across India, Europe, the US, and Asia-Pacific.
